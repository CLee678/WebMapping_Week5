US Electric Power Generation

Comparison of the Produced Power

Map authored by Cayley, Wing Yee Lee

The principles I used are "Choose a suitable map library", "Use relative units and breakpoints" and "Test and debug your map design".
"Choose a suitable map library": I changed to Mapbox basemap as it shows the state boundaries and state names. The color of the basemap is light, making the circle markers stand out more.
"Use relative units and breakpoints": I changed the capacity from MW to percentages. Using percentages as a relative unit is more intuitive for people to understand whether it is a large/small amount of capacity produced.
"Test and debug your map design": For the small circle markers, it was difficult to click, and they did not zoom in to the respective locations. I changed the weight, and I added a 'click' and 'flyTo' method so that upon clicking, the location zooms in to the center of the map. The zoom in is slow, and I initially used the "setView" method, but the circle marker didn't show in the center of the map, so I switched to "flyTo".
In response to the professor's feedback, I adjusted the solar plants to be more transparent."
